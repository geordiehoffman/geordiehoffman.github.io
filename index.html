About Me

I am a graduate student in the anthropology department at the University of Utah, researching the intersection of gender equality, market integration, and health.

Current Projects

Evolution of Obesity in Human Societies

Using ethnographic photographs and machine learning to analyze when obesity became widespread in small scale societies. BMI is predicted from facial images using Vision Transformers (ViT). Changes in population BMI trends are compared with dates and identified markers of market integration (e.g., mass-manufactured clothing, sunglasses, cars) to predict what types of lifestyle changes had the largest impact on body size and related health risks.

Sexual Division of Labor Across Economic Systems

A literary review of time allocation data from 71 societies, ranging from hunter-gatherers to fully commercial economies, to quantify labor division by sex. The study accounts for childcare and highlights how gendered work patterns vary across economic formations.
